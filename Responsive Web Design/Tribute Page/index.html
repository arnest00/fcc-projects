Facts about Angkor Wat

  • The name it goes by today means "Temple City" or "City of Temples" in Khmer. When it was originally constructed, it was known as Vrah Viṣṇuloka in Sanskrit, or "the sacred dwelling of Vishnu."
  • Angkor Wat was built between 1113 to 1150 AD. By the end of construction, 5 to 10 million sandstone blocks weighing up to 1.5 tons each would be used to build it.
  • Decorating the temple's architecture is a series of bas-reliefs depicting scenes from Hindu epics.
  • The complex's main tower aligns with the sun on the morning of the Spring Equinox.
  • The city of Angkor, where the temple complex stands, was recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1992.
  • Today, Angkor Wat is a major tourist site. In 1993, less than 10,000 people visited the site. Twenty years later, the number of tourists would swell up to 2 million.
